# Evidence Foundry - Invitation to Review

For academics, university administrators, graduate students, research users, and researcher-developers invited to evaluate an early research workspace.

This is not a sales pitch. There is nothing to buy. Evidence Foundry is in reviewed-access beta, and we are looking for serious feedback from people who understand scholarly work, graduate training, research supervision, and institutional trust.

Blunt criticism is more useful than encouragement.

- Public site: https://evidencefoundry.com
- Workspace sign-in: https://app.evidencefoundry.com
- Contact: bot@evidencefoundry.com

## What Evidence Foundry Is

Evidence Foundry is a truth-first research workspace.

Its purpose is to help researchers keep sources, evidence, claims, writing, review, and export connected. A major scholarly claim should trace to one of three things:

- the researcher's own evidence;
- a real source or citation;
- an explicitly marked hypothesis, limitation, or open question.

The system is not meant to replace supervisors, committees, reviewers, or scholarly judgment. It is meant to make that judgment easier to inspect.

AI may assist with drafting, summarizing, critique, and gap-finding. It may not invent the science, silently upgrade weak evidence, or turn an unsupported claim into a supported one.

In one sentence: Evidence Foundry makes scholarly work more auditable, not more automated.

## How Access Works

Evidence Foundry is currently reviewed-access beta.

1. Go to https://evidencefoundry.com
2. Click **Apply for reviewed access**.
3. Submit your name, institution or organization, role, email, and a short note about the research workflow you want to test.
4. If approved, you will receive an access email from `bot@evidencefoundry.com`.
5. That email will guide you through passkey setup.
6. After setup, sign in at https://app.evidencefoundry.com

Please use a passkey-capable browser such as a current version of Chrome, Safari, or Edge.

## What To Try

Use non-sensitive, sample, synthetic, public, or explicitly approved material only.

A useful first test is small:

1. Create or open a project.
2. Add one or two sources.
3. Add a small piece of evidence, a sample result, or a public dataset excerpt.
4. Create several claims.
5. Link each claim to source or evidence support.
6. Write a short results, discussion, or argument section.
7. Run the rigor or claim review.
8. Export the package.

The point is not whether the beta workspace contains your whole research life. The point is whether the loop makes sense:

**sources and evidence -> claims -> writing -> review -> export**

## What We Want Feedback On

Please focus on whether the foundation is intellectually useful and institutionally credible.

Key questions:

- Does the core workflow make scholarly sense?
- Would graduate students understand the difference between a claim they declare and support the system computes?
- Does the workflow reduce bad scholarly habits, or does it add too much friction?
- What would a supervisor, committee, reviewer, or department need to trust an exported package?
- What would a university require before a controlled pilot: privacy, governance, access control, hosting, data retention, auditability, or legal review?
- What should not be built yet?
- Where does the language overclaim?
- What would make this genuinely useful for dissertations, papers, lab reports, or research training?

## Specific Feedback For Administrators

If you are reviewing from an institutional or administrative perspective, we are especially interested in:

- whether the access model is clear enough for a small controlled beta;
- what data-governance language is missing;
- what a university would need before allowing real student or lab use;
- whether this could support graduate research training or supervision;
- whether student researcher-developers could help build field-specific branches safely.

## Specific Feedback For Researcher-Developers

Evidence Foundry is the trunk workspace. Field-specific tools can become branches when they produce evidence objects that the workspace can import, review, and export.

Sensarra, a biosensor/electrochemistry branch, is one early example. It is not the whole project.

If you build research software, analysis pipelines, lab tools, data workflows, or field-specific methods, please tell us:

- what kind of evidence your tool produces;
- whether it could emit portable evidence records;
- what claims a researcher would make from that evidence;
- what rigor checks would be needed before those claims should appear in a paper or dissertation.

## Important Data Note

This is an early hosted beta. Do not upload confidential, human-subjects, legally restricted, institution-restricted, commercially sensitive, or unpublished sensitive data unless it has been explicitly approved for this evaluation.

For now, test with:

- synthetic examples;
- public sources;
- non-sensitive research material;
- sample lab or classroom material;
- material you are explicitly comfortable using in an early software evaluation.

Longer term, the goal is institution-appropriate research infrastructure: portable exports, clear provenance, no lock-in, and deployment/governance options suitable for serious research environments.

## What A Good Review Looks Like

A useful response can be short. For example:

- "This loop makes sense, but students will not understand the status labels."
- "The export package would be useful if it included X."
- "A department would reject this until Y is clarified."
- "This is not useful for my field because Z."
- "The most promising use is graduate methods training, not paper writing."
- "Do not build live connectors yet; fix the review/export loop first."

We are trying to learn what must be true before Evidence Foundry deserves to be used in real scholarly work.

## Why We Are Asking

We are not trying to validate a quick writing app. We are testing whether a truth-first research workspace is a real piece of scholarly infrastructure: something that could help students, researchers, supervisors, and institutions keep work defensible in a world where AI-generated text is becoming cheap and trust is becoming expensive.

If that problem matters to you, we would value your review.
