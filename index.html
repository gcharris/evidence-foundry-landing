01
The working rule

A manuscript is a chain of claims. Each link must hold.

In Evidence Foundry, every major scholarly assertion traces to one of three admissible groundings — and the workspace keeps the trace.

Grounding · I

Your own evidence

Evidence objects imported from your lab's own pipelines — datasets, analyses, validated results — filed with provenance and linked to the claims they support.

Grounding · II

A real source

Citations that resolve to literature actually held in your source library — so the reference you cite is the reference you read.

Grounding · III

A declared open question

When the evidence is not yet in, the claim is marked as a hypothesis or open question — visibly, in the record and in the draft.

AI may assist — draft, summarize, critique, review. It does not invent the science, and it cannot upgrade a claim's evidence status. Standing is a question of records, not rhetoric.

House rule · applies to every assistant in the workspace
02
The instrument

One loop, kept in order.

The workspace carries the work from record to manuscript without losing the thread between them.

№ Stage What it holds
01 Source library The literature you actually hold — references, metadata, and the reading record behind every citation.
02 Evidence inbox Evidence objects imported from field pipelines — data, analyses, results — retained with their provenance.
03 Claim cards Each major assertion becomes a record: its wording, its status, and the evidence and sources that carry it.
04 Claim-linked writing Drafts cite the record. As sections grow, the claim map grows with them.
05 Rigor review Deterministic checks surface unsupported claims and broken links before a committee or reviewer does.
06 Export Papers and dissertation chapters leave with a claim map and a canonical manifest of what stands on what.
Claim status vocabulary Grounded Contested Unsupported Declared open

Status is computed from the links on record — not asserted by the author, and never by the assistant.

03
Who it serves

Graduate students through faculty.

Evidence Foundry is the trunk workspace: field-specific analysis tools produce the evidence, and their outputs import here to become defensible scholarship. One early branch analyzes electrochemical biosensor data; the trunk itself is discipline-neutral.

For

Doctoral candidates

A dissertation is years of claims. Keep every chapter's assertions tied to the record that will be examined — and know what is still open before the committee asks.

For

Lab groups

Instrument pipelines keep producing results; the workspace keeps them citable. Evidence lands with provenance and stays attached to the writing it supports.

For

Supervisors & faculty

Review a chapter by its claim map: what is grounded, what is contested, what is honestly declared open. The rigor check reads the record, not the prose.

For

Researcher-developers

Field branches are how instruments join the trunk: a pipeline that emits evidence objects can feed the workspace. If you want to build one for your method, say so in your application.
